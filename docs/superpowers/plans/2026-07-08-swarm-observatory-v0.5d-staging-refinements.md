## Rulings (all pre-adjudicated by the v0.5c gates)

1. **Aspect-aware lead** (critic n1): the ≤0.75 xFrac ceiling holds at 1280×720 (0.755) and full-width drawers (0.706) but breaks in the flanked-narrow band — 1081–1160px windows, canvas ≈625px, peak 0.809. Cause: the lead is a world-unit offset calibrated at one aspect; a narrower canvas has a smaller horizontal half-frame, so the same world offset is a larger fraction. Remedy: scale the effective lead by the live canvas aspect relative to the calibration aspect (`leadEff = FOLLOW_LEAD · min(1, aspect/CALIB_ASPECT)` or the equivalent the implementer derives — pure, tested) **[CORRECTION — v0.5d T1/T2, both reviewers confirmed: this illustrative sketch is DIRECTIONALLY WRONG — it REDUCES the lead on a narrower canvas, pushing the head further toward the clipping edge. The shipped/derived form is `leadEff = FOLLOW_LEAD + gain·(1 − min(1, aspect/CALIB_ASPECT))` — MORE lead on a narrower canvas (see task-v05d-1-report.md §1). Do not copy the sketch.]**, then re-run the critic's three-aspect sweep (1280×720, ~900px full-width, ~1120px flanked-narrow) with target ≤0.75 at ALL of them and label-never-clips. Canvas aspect is available from the renderer/camera per frame (number read, zero-alloc).
2. **Screen-space-constant tracking ring** (critic n2): the fixed world-scale-8 ring dies in the late climb (~ticks 38–46) exactly where the head is most sub-pixel. Remedy: the TRACKING arm's scale becomes distance-proportional — `scale = RING_TRACK_K · distance(camera, head)` with min/max clamps (floor ≈ the current 8-at-calibration-distance equivalence; ceiling to keep it a marker, not a target — derive both, comment the derivation). Selection/finale arms keep scale 1 exactly (no leakage — the v0.5c explicit-set-per-arm shape stays).
3. **Ring persists through mid-run pause** (critic n3 + fable A2): pause-then-click is the sanctioned mid-run selection path, but the ring hides on pause — clickable yet undiscoverable. Remedy: the tracking gate becomes `(playing || pausedMidRun) && unselected && positioned && bounds !== null && !finale`, where `pausedMidRun = !playing && 0 < tick < tickCount`. Cold rest (tick 0, never-played or scrubbed-to-0) and the natural-end rest stay quiet (finale owns the end; the cold open stays unadorned). Grammar sentence: the marker shows wherever an unselected subject is mid-run — playing or paused. Update `shouldTrackWithRing` (signature grows), its truth table, the gate-matrix tests, AND the smoke expectation that pinned pause→hidden (consciously flip it with the ruling cited — this is the one test amendment of the cycle).
4. **Finale swap transient** (critic n4): one naked-head beat at the tracking→finale 8→1 swap while the camera is still wide. Remedy: ease the ring scale across the swap (dt-eased, T1 helpers, RM snaps) OR defer the swap until the finale ease has progressed (implementer picks the minimal §8-clean mechanism; the critic's bar is "no naked-head beat visible at 100ms sampling").
5. **Re-fit tail** (critic n5): the scrub-from-finale ease lands ~85% during the drag — reads as a whip. Remedy: the establish-refit consume uses a slightly gentler rate (a dedicated constant derived from the focus rate, e.g. ×0.5–0.7 — calibrate so the settle reads as a camera move; the critic's numbers were 47.7→0 u/100ms over ~500ms; target a visibly longer deceleration without lag-feel). Scoped to the REFIT path only — tour-arrival and plain establish keep the current rate (their feel passed two gates).
6. **Tour-start camera reset** (critic RULING, its words: "restores the direction, not touches it"): `useTour.start()` — after its bracket, snap/ease the camera to the composed default vantage the choreography assumes (the CameraRig load framing: `frameFor(bounds, FIT_MAX_FACTOR opts)` result, or `frameFor(null)` when bounds null). Instant cut is acceptable (tour-start is an explicit stage-take; RM parity trivial) — implementer may use a fast ease if it reads better, but step-0's caption must open on the correct vantage in EVERY entry state (from finale rest, from mid-run orbit, from cold). Plain-tour A/B must be pixel-equivalent (reset is a no-op when already at the vantage). This touches useTour.ts — the ONLY sanctioned tour-file change; everything else in the tour stays byte-identical.
